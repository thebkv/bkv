## Odyssey → Psychological State → Bible Inner-State Parallels (Merged & Corrected)

| Odyssey Name | Psychological State | Inner Meaning | Bible Name / Figure (Inner State Match) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Odysseus | Fragmented self seeking reintegration | The many-turned man whose identity has been scattered by experience and must be gathered back into wholeness. | David — the divided heart moving toward restored kingship (alt: Jacob — the sanctified trickster who secures inheritance through cunning and struggle) |
| Penelope | Faithful inner center | The stable core that preserves identity during absence and chaos. | Abigail — wise, steady discernment that restrains destruction |
| Telemachus | Emerging identity | The future self awakening and searching for authority and orientation. | Samuel — the faculty learning to hear and stand upright |
| Mentor | Guiding intelligence | Counsel that forms without dominating; wisdom in disguise. | Nathan — corrective guidance that restores truth |
| Athena | Strategic ordering intellect (metis) | Discernment that plans, disguises, tests, and restores through intelligent action. | Lady Wisdom (Sophia) — architectural, shrewd wisdom that builds the house |
| Polyphemus | Lawless ego / monocular force | One-eyed consciousness: power without law, reciprocity, or spiritual sight. | Goliath — brute force divorced from covenant |
| Circe | Enslaving pleasure | Desire that devolves the human (Divine Image) into appetite. | Delilah — seduction that weakens vocation |
| Calypso | Forgetful stagnation | Comfort that conceals purpose and delays return. | Lot — settling into ease that arrests calling |
| Sirens | Destructive fascination | Desire or knowledge that promises fulfillment but annihilates agency. | False Prophets — seductive voices leading away from life |
| Scylla | Inevitable loss | Necessary sacrifice; damage that cannot be avoided on the narrow path. | Job — loss endured without total collapse |
| Charybdis | Total annihilation | Absolute collapse to be avoided even at cost. | Pharaoh — destructive rigidity that consumes itself |
| Lotus-Eaters | Escapist numbness | Addiction to forgetfulness; surrender of memory and aim. | Esau — trading inheritance for immediate relief |
| Underworld | Confrontation with mortality | Facing limits, loss, and irreversible truth. | Sheol — encounter with death and silence |
| Ithaca | Integrated self | Home as restored order; identity reconciled and at rest. | Jerusalem — the inner city of peace |
| Troy | Past conflict | The finished outer battle whose inner consequences remain unresolved. | Egypt — former bondage shaping present wounds |
| Phaiakia | Transitional restoration | Healing hospitality that prepares the self for final return. | Wilderness — reorientation before possession |
