# Ephesians 00 — Introduction  
## *The Book of Ephesians: Unity, Identity, and the New Humanity*

---

# **1. Historical Setting**

### **Author**
Traditionally attributed to **Paul**, the apostle and missionary to the Gentiles.  
Ephesians carries Paul’s unmistakable voice: elevated, cosmic, deeply theological, yet pastoral and practical.

### **Where It Was Written**
Likely written from **Rome**, during Paul’s first imprisonment (around **AD 60–62**).  
In his letters from captivity, Paul often writes with clarity, solemnity, and a sweeping sense of spiritual vision — Ephesians exemplifies this tone.

### **Audience**
Addressed to believers in **Ephesus**, an influential city in Asia Minor (modern-day Turkey).  
Ephesus was:

- a major port city  
- a cultural and commercial center  
- home to the Temple of Artemis (one of the Seven Wonders)  
- a spiritual crossroads filled with philosophical schools, mystery religions, and magical practices  

The Ephesian church was a mix of Jewish and Gentile disciples living in a spiritually charged environment.

### **Situation**
Paul is:

- **physically confined**,  
- **spiritually expansive**,  
- and writing to stabilize, encourage, and elevate a church dealing with pressure from culture, internal division, and competing spiritual claims.

Ephesians reads less like a letter handling a crisis and more like a **manifesto** — a declaration of the believer’s identity and destiny.

---

# **2. The Message of Ephesians (Outer Meaning)**

Ephesians contains two major movements:

### **Chapters 1–3 — Revelation (What God Has Done)**
Paul reveals:

- the eternal plan of God  
- the believer’s identity in Christ  
- the unification of Jew and Gentile  
- the mystery hidden from ages and now revealed  
- the resurrection power available to the believer  

This section is cosmic in scale, breathtaking, and foundational.

### **Chapters 4–6 — Transformation (How to Walk It Out)**
Paul then turns to:

- unity of the Body  
- spiritual gifts  
- maturity in love  
- the difference between the old and new humanity  
- household relationships  
- and the full armor of God  

It is practical, relational, and anchored in daily life.

---

# **3. The BKV Reading: Symbolic & Inner Meaning**

Ephesians is one of the most “inner-friendly” books in the New Testament.  
Its language is already symbolic, elevated, and metaphysical.

## **Core BKV Themes**

### **1. Identity (Chs. 1–2)**
Ephesians reveals:

- who you actually are  
- why you exist  
- what God intended **before** the world was formed  
- and what spiritual inheritance has already been given  

“Chosen before the foundation of the world” becomes a symbol of the **true identity** that precedes the ego.

### **2. Unity (Chs. 2–4)**
Jew and Gentile becoming “one new man” symbolizes:

- the inner integration of divided faculties  
- the healing of inner dualities  
- the restoration of a unified inner state under Christ  

Christ as **Head** symbolizes the governing center of the redeemed self.

### **3. Transformation (Chs. 4–6)**
“Walking worthy” becomes the daily expression of your rising consciousness.

Putting off the **“old man”** symbolizes:

- ego patterns  
- unregulated drives  
- hardened understanding  

Putting on the **“new man”** symbolizes:

- the recreated inner self  
- the soul aligned with Divine Life  
- the inner image restored in Christ  

### **4. Spiritual Warfare (Ch. 6)**
The armor of God represents:

- protection of consciousness  
- ordering of inner faculties  
- clarity, truth, righteousness, stability, and faith  

“Principalities and powers” symbolize the **archetypal forces** that influence the psyche and the world.

---

# **4. Why Ephesians Matters in the BKV Framework**

Ephesians is the **architectural book** of the New Testament.  
It reveals the structural blueprint of the inner life:

- the plan (Ch. 1)  
- the construction (Ch. 2–3)  
- the walk (Ch. 4–5)  
- the warfare (Ch. 6)  

Where Romans deals with **process**, Ephesians deals with **position**.

Where Galatians deals with **freedom**, Ephesians deals with **identity**.

Where Corinthians deals with **problems**, Ephesians deals with **perfection**.

In BKV terms:

**Ephesians teaches the disciple who they are,  
what they contain,  
and how to walk as the new humanity in Christ.**

---

# **5. The Spiritual Tone of the Book**

Ephesians is majestic.  
It sounds like someone writing from a prison,  
but seeing a universe.

It begins in eternity (“before the foundation of the world”)  
and ends in warfare (“having done all, stand”).  
It is the perfect fusion of:

- theology  
- devotion  
- symbol  
- identity  
- exhortation  
- cosmic vision  

Ephesians refuses to let the disciple think small.

---

# **Summary: What Ephesians 00 Establishes**

- **Historical:** Paul, imprisoned in Rome, writes to a spiritually diverse church in Ephesus (AD 60–62).  
- **Structural:** Two movements — Revelation (1–3) and Transformation (4–6).  
- **Symbolic:** Identity, integration, new humanity, Christ as Head, spiritual architecture.  
- **Purpose:** To show the disciple who they are in Christ — and how to walk as that new person.
