## Text

**1** The [Lord](#lord) is my [shepherd](#shepherd); I shall not [want](#want).

**2** He maketh me to lie down in [green pastures](#green-pastures): he leadeth me beside the [still waters](#still-waters).

**3** He restoreth my [soul](#soul): he leadeth me in the [paths of righteousness](#paths-of-righteousness) for his [name's sake](#names-sake).

**4** Yea, though I walk through the [valley](#valley) of the [shadow of death](#shadow-of-death), I will fear no [evil](#evil): for thou art with me; thy [rod](#rod) and thy [staff](#staff) they comfort me.

**5** Thou preparest a [table](#table) before me in the presence of mine [enemies](#enemies): thou anointest my [head](#head) with [oil](#oil); my [cup](#cup) runneth over.

**6** Surely [goodness](#goodness) and [mercy](#mercy) shall follow me all the days of my life: and I will dwell in the [house of the Lord](#house-of-the-lord) for ever.

---

# Summary

Psalm 23 is not merely a comfort poem. In BKV terms, it is a complete disciple map. The soul is led from dependence, to restoration, to right walking, through the valley of apparent death, into prepared communion, until consciousness is settled in the house of the Lord.

The chapter begins with guidance and ends with dwelling. It moves from being led to belonging.

---

# Core Principle

The disciple is not sustained by force, anxiety, or self-management. The disciple is shepherded by the Lord. When the inner life yields to that guidance, lack is answered, fear is displaced, enemies lose authority, and the soul returns to its proper dwelling.

# Transformation Pattern

1. **Recognition** - The Lord is my shepherd.  
2. **Provision** - I shall not want.  
3. **Restoration** - He restoreth my soul.  
4. **Right Walking** - He leadeth me in paths of righteousness.  
5. **Passage Through Fear** - I walk through the valley of the shadow of death.  
6. **Presence** - Thou art with me.  
7. **Communion** - Thou preparest a table before me.  
8. **Consecration** - Thou anointest my head with oil.  
9. **Overflow** - My cup runneth over.  
10. **Dwelling** - I will dwell in the house of the Lord.

---

# Believer Message

The Lord cares for His own. He provides, restores, protects, comforts, and brings the believer into His presence. The psalm is a promise of divine care in every condition of life.

---

# Disciple Message

The disciple must consent to being led. Psalm 23 is not only a promise of comfort. It is a pattern of yielded movement. The disciple follows the Shepherd through provision, restoration, righteousness, valley, table, anointing, overflow, and dwelling.
