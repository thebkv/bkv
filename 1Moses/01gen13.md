# Dossier: Genesis 13  
**Subject: Separation for the Preservation of Peace**

---

## BKV Spine

### Spine Summary

Genesis 13 addresses the first internal conflict produced by blessing. Alignment has brought increase, but growth now creates friction. The chapter reveals that not all separation is loss. Some separation is required to preserve peace, clarity, and continued movement. Abram demonstrates that trust does not cling to advantage, territory, or priority. He yields choice because alignment does not depend on control. This chapter establishes that preservation of relationship and orientation matters more than immediate gain.

### Disciple Orientation

• Blessing can introduce pressure as well as provision.  
• Conflict often reveals competing orientations rather than scarcity.  
• Voluntary separation can preserve alignment.  
• Trust releases control without fear of loss.

## SDR / RFM — Genesis 13  
**Choosing Peace Over Control**

For the disciple, Genesis 13 reveals how trust behaves when blessing increases.

Abram and Lot are both prospering. Nothing is wrong on the surface. Yet conflict emerges, not because of evil, but because growth has outpaced shared capacity. Alignment now faces a decision.

Abram does not assert seniority or claim entitlement. He releases control. He allows Lot to choose first. This is not weakness. It is confidence that provision does not depend on grasping.

Lot chooses by sight. The land looks good. The outcome will prove costly. Abram chooses by trust. He waits for instruction rather than advantage.

Only after separation does Source speak again. Promise expands once peace is preserved. The land is reaffirmed. Movement continues.

Genesis 13 teaches the disciple that peace is sometimes protected by distance. Letting go can be the very act that secures what was promised.

Trust does not compete.
