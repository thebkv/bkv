# Dossier: Genesis 04  
**Subject: Fractured Alignment Expressed Through Rivalry and Violence**

---

## BKV Spine

### Spine Summary

Genesis 4 shows how misalignment spreads beyond the self and into relationship. What fractured internally in Genesis 3 now manifests socially. Desire turns competitive, comparison replaces trust, and resentment seeks justification. Worship becomes transactional, responsibility is rejected, and violence emerges as the attempt to resolve inner disorder externally. This chapter reveals that unaddressed misalignment does not remain private. It multiplies.

### Disciple Orientation

• Inner disorder eventually expresses itself outwardly.  
• Comparison corrodes alignment.  
• Resentment grows when correction is resisted.  
• Violence begins as a refusal to master desire.

## SDR / RFM — Genesis 04  
**When Misalignment Multiplies**

For the disciple, Genesis 4 explains how inner disorder becomes relational damage.

Cain and Abel bring offerings, but what is revealed is not the gift. It is the heart behind it. One offering flows from trust. The other seeks recognition through effort. When correction is offered, Cain is invited to re-orient. Nothing forces the outcome.

Desire presses for control. Cain refuses to master it. Violence follows. What could have been addressed inwardly is acted out outwardly.

Afterward, responsibility is denied. The fracture widens. Consequence unfolds, yet destruction is restrained. Life continues, but alignment does not return.

Civilization grows. Tools advance. Power increases. But alignment decays further. Progress without restoration only magnifies instability.

Yet the story does not end there. A new line is given. Seth appears, not as a reward, but as preservation. Calling continues. Alignment remains possible.

Genesis 4 teaches the disciple that unresolved misalignment always spreads. Restoration begins not by overpowering others, but by mastering desire and returning to Source orientation.

Alignment is still offered.
