# Dossier: Genesis 26  
**Subject: Promise Preserved Through Repetition**

---

## BKV Spine

### Spine Summary

Genesis 26 shows promise preserved through repetition rather than novelty. Isaac encounters famine, fear, and conflict much like Abraham did. The chapter reveals that inheritance includes patterns as well as blessing. Isaac does not innovate; he remains aligned within established instruction. Wells are contested, reopened, and named again. The chapter establishes that faithfulness often looks like staying steady when circumstances repeat.

### Disciple Orientation

• Inherited promise includes inherited tests.  
• Faithfulness may require repetition, not originality.  
• Conflict does not cancel blessing.  
• Persistence outlasts opposition.

## SDR / RFM — Genesis 26  
**Faithfulness That Stays**

For the disciple, Genesis 26 is about endurance.

Isaac inherits promise, but he also inherits patterns. Fear resurfaces. Conflict repeats. Wells must be dug again. Nothing feels dramatic, yet alignment holds.

Isaac does not seize land by force. He keeps moving until space is made. Eventually peace follows persistence.

Genesis 26 teaches the disciple that faithfulness is often quiet endurance through familiar trials. Promise is preserved by staying aligned, not by escaping repetition.

What remains yields fruit.
