# Dossier: Genesis 15  
**Subject: Trust Counted and Covenant Established**

---

## BKV Spine

### Spine Summary

Genesis 15 formalizes the relationship between trust and promise. After rescue and refusal, Abram is given assurance without demand for performance. The chapter establishes that trust, not achievement, is what secures alignment. Promise is clarified, fear is addressed, and covenant is enacted unilaterally by Source. Abram does not negotiate terms or initiate action. He receives assurance and believes. This chapter defines faith as reliance on Source commitment rather than human capacity.

### Disciple Orientation

• Trust precedes fulfillment and does not require proof.  
• Fear is addressed by promise, not by control.  
• Covenant is enacted by Source, not enforced by effort.  
• Alignment rests on belief rather than performance.

## SDR / RFM — Genesis 15  
**Trust That Rests**

For the disciple, Genesis 15 shows what happens when trust stops striving.

Abram has acted bravely. He has refused corruption. Now he waits. Fear surfaces. Source responds, not with instruction, but with reassurance.

Abram is shown the stars. No timeline is given. No mechanism is explained. He is simply asked to trust. And he does. That trust is counted as right alignment. Nothing is built. Nothing is conquered. Yet everything is secured.

Then something remarkable happens. Abram falls into deep stillness. The covenant is enacted without him walking the path. Source alone passes through. Responsibility does not rest on human consistency, but on divine commitment.

Genesis 15 teaches the disciple that alignment sometimes looks like rest. Trust is not always active movement. Sometimes it is receiving assurance and allowing Source to carry the weight.

The promise stands because Source stands.
