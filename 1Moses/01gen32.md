# Dossier: Genesis 32  
**Subject: Confrontation and Renaming**

---

## BKV Spine

### Spine Summary  
Genesis 32 records the moment where identity can no longer advance under its former name. Forward movement stalls until unresolved fear, reliance on strategy, and divided strength are exposed. Alignment is pressed not through external threat, but through direct confrontation with Source. Authority does not emerge by winning, but by surrendering false strength and receiving a redefined orientation.

### Disciple Orientation  
• Preparation cannot replace transformation.  
• Fear reveals where identity is still divided.  
• Alignment is not secured by strategy, but by encounter.  
• A limp received from truth is stronger than strength preserved by avoidance.

## SDR / RFM — *The Night of Renaming*

For the disciple, Genesis 32 is where progress halts until truth is faced.

Jacob has promises, history, and confirmation, but he still carries fear. He prepares, plans, divides, negotiates, and prays. None of it moves him forward. Only when he is left alone does the real confrontation begin.

The struggle is not with Esau. It is with the identity that learned to survive by grasping, managing, and outmaneuvering. That identity cannot inherit what is next.

The touch that disables Jacob does not destroy him. It frees him. The limp becomes evidence that alignment has replaced control.

When the name changes, direction changes. Israel moves forward not as a strategist, but as one who has encountered Source and been reoriented.

Genesis 32 teaches the disciple that blessing is not seized. It is received when false strength is surrendered.
