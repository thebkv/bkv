# Dossier: Genesis 14  
**Subject: Authority Revealed Through Rescue and Refusal**

---

## BKV Spine

### Spine Summary

Genesis 14 introduces conflict beyond internal tension and tests alignment under external threat. Abram enters armed confrontation not for conquest, but for rescue. The chapter reveals the difference between rightful authority and opportunistic power. Victory does not legitimize possession. Blessing is received from a higher authority, while reward from corrupt systems is refused. This chapter establishes that aligned authority acts decisively in crisis but remains uncontaminated by the systems it confronts.

### Disciple Orientation

• Alignment does not avoid conflict when rescue is required.  
• Authority is demonstrated by restraint after victory.  
• Blessing flows from Source, not from conquest.  
• Refusal of corrupt reward preserves integrity.

## SDR / RFM — Genesis 14  
**Victory Without Ownership**

For the disciple, Genesis 14 clarifies how alignment behaves under pressure.

Abram does not seek conflict. Conflict comes to him. When Lot is taken, alignment does not hesitate. Rescue is decisive, focused, and limited. Victory is achieved, but it is not exploited.

Afterward, two authorities approach Abram. One offers blessing. The other offers profit. Abram discerns the difference. He receives from the priest of the Most High. He refuses the reward of a system built on domination and loss.

This refusal matters. It preserves clarity of source. Abram will not allow his identity to be defined by what he took, only by whom he trusts.

Genesis 14 teaches the disciple that true authority can win battles without becoming what it defeats. Rescue does not require ownership. Victory does not require contamination.

Alignment answers to a higher order.
