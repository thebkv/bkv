# Genesis 32
## Confrontation and Renaming

---

## Spine Summary

Genesis 32 records the moment where identity can no longer advance under its former name.  
Forward movement stalls until fear, strategy, and divided strength are exposed.  
Alignment is pressed through direct encounter with Source.  
Authority emerges by surrendering false strength and receiving a redefined orientation.

---

## Disciple Orientation

- Preparation cannot replace transformation.
- Fear reveals where identity is still divided.
- Alignment is not secured by strategy, but by encounter.
- A limp received from truth is stronger than strength preserved by avoidance.

---

## Genesis 32:1–2

Movement resumes, but confirmation appears before confrontation.

---

## Genesis 32:3–8

External threat exposes unresolved inner division.  
Strategy attempts preservation through fragmentation.

---

## Genesis 32:22–24

Isolation removes all external leverage.  
Resistance emerges from Source, not circumstance.

---

## Genesis 32:27–28

Identity must be named before it can be transformed.  
Orientation shifts from self-reliance to alignment.

---

## Symbolics

- Night: Unseen internal arena of confrontation.
- Wrestling: Sustained resistance between fear and trust.
- Limp: Strength replaced by dependence.
- Israel: Identity defined by alignment.

---

## The Night of Renaming

Progress halts until false strength collapses.  
Alignment advances only after surrender.
