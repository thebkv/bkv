# Genesis 26 — Preservation Through Integrity Under Pressure
## MSR Architectural Reading

## MSR Preface (Genesis 26–Specific)

Genesis 26 revisits earlier Genesis patterns **without advancing lineage**.  
Under **Minimum Sufficient Role (MSR)**, this chapter exists to demonstrate that:

> **Alignment can be preserved without novelty, conquest, or expansion.**

Isaac does not invent a new path.  
He **re-holds** the pattern already revealed.

This chapter answers a critical MSR question:
What happens when the promise passes to a quieter, less dramatic bearer?

---

## Core MSR Function of Genesis 26

Genesis 26 tests whether **inheritance alone** is sufficient to sustain alignment.

It proves that:
- Blessing is maintained by **consistency**, not originality
- Conflict is resolved by **non-domination**
- Authority is preserved through **yielding without surrender**

## MSR Summary — Genesis 26

Genesis 26 teaches that:

- Alignment can be inherited but must be **maintained**
- Fear may recur without collapsing the structure
- Yielding preserves authority more effectively than force
- Blessing provokes resistance, but restraint produces space
- Peace arrives after stability proves genuine

Isaac does not advance the promise.
He **keeps it intact**.

That is his role.
