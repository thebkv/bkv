# The Rendering (BKV-MSR)

> **1** Therefore we must give the more earnest attention to what we have heard, lest we gradually drift away from it. **2** For if the message declared through Angels and guiding principles proved steadfast, and every transgression and disobedience received its fitting consequence, **3** how shall we escape if we neglect so great a salvation? This salvation was first declared by the Lord, was confirmed to us by those who heard Him, **4** while God also bore witness by signs, wonders, various works of power, and distributions of the Holy Spirit according to His own purpose.
>
> **5** For it was not to Angels or subordinate spiritual agencies that He subjected the world to come, about which we are speaking. **6** But one has testified somewhere, saying,
>
> *“What is man, that You are mindful of him,*
>
> *or the son of man, that You care for him?*
>
> **7** *You made him for a little while lower than the Angels;*
>
> *You crowned him with glory and honor,*
>
> *and appointed him over the works of Your hands.*
>
> **8** *You put all things in subjection beneath his feet.”*
>
> In placing everything under him, God left nothing outside his intended dominion. Yet at present we do not see all things brought into that harmony.
>
> **9** But we see Jesus, who for a little while was made lower than the Angels, now crowned with glory and honor because He suffered death, so that by the grace of God He might taste death on behalf of everyone.
>
> **10** For it was fitting that He, for whom and through whom all things exist, in bringing many sons into glory, should make the Captain and Pioneer of their salvation complete through suffering.
>
> **11** For both the One who sanctifies and those who are being sanctified share one origin. Therefore He is not ashamed to call them brothers and sisters, **12** saying,
>
> *“I will declare Your name to My brethren;*
>
> *in the midst of the assembly I will sing Your praise.”*
>
> **13** And again,
>
> *“I will put My trust in Him.”*
>
> And again,
>
> *“Behold, I and the children whom God has given Me.”*
>
> **14** Therefore, since the children share in flesh and blood, He Himself likewise fully shared in the same human condition, so that through death He might render powerless the one who held the dominion of death—that is, the adversary—and **15** might free those who, through fear of death, were held in lifelong bondage.
>
> **16** For surely it is not Angels that He helps, but the seed of Abraham—the lineage of faith and promise. **17** Therefore He had to become like His brothers and sisters in every respect, so that He might become a merciful and faithful High Priest in the things pertaining to God, making reconciliation and restoration for the estrangement of the people.
>
> **18** Because He Himself has suffered while being tested, He is able to come to the aid of those who are undergoing testing.
