# Genesis 25 — Succession, Differentiation, and the Transfer of Promise
## MSR Architectural Reading

---

## MSR Preface (Genesis 25–Specific)

Genesis 25 records **the closing of one authority cycle and the emergence of differentiated trajectories**.

Under MSR, this chapter is not primarily about:
- chronology
- genealogy for its own sake
- favoritism or personal drama

It functions to **separate lines of inheritance**, establish **legitimate continuity**, and introduce **internal division that requires discernment**.

What matters is not who is born —  
but **which line carries authority forward**.

---

## Core MSR Principle for Genesis 25

> **Promise advances by ordered succession, not by numerical strength.**

Multiplicity does not equal continuity.

## MSR Synthesis — Genesis 25

Genesis 25 establishes that:

- Authority is **transferred intentionally**
- Multiplicity must be **separated to preserve function**
- Internal conflict reveals **competing trajectories**
- Long-range promise is incompatible with short-range appetite

This chapter does not condemn Esau —  
it shows **why some lines cannot carry continuity forward**.
