# Genesis 27 — Authority Claimed Through Deception
## MSR Architectural Reading (Two-Column Comparison)

---

## MSR Preface (Genesis 27)

Genesis 27 is not about favoritism, ethnic destiny, or moral approval of deceit.

Under **Minimum Sufficient Role (MSR)**, this chapter exposes a **misaligned transfer of authority**:
authority pursued **by outcome**, not **by order**.

The chapter functions as a *stress test*:
What happens when blessing is seized through **control and manipulation** rather than **trust and alignment**?

MSR reads this chapter by **function**, not excuse.
No character is idealized.
Every role is revealed.

---

## Core MSR Principle (Genesis 27)

> **Authority obtained without alignment produces instability, even when outcomes appear correct.**

## MSR Summary (Genesis 27)

Genesis 27 reveals that:

- Authority can be transferred improperly  
- Outcomes can align while process fails  
- Blessing once released cannot be recalled  
- Deception accelerates results but fractures trust  
- Separation becomes necessary when order is violated  

This chapter does **not** justify deception.  
It exposes its cost.

---

## Minimum Sufficient Role Statement

**Genesis 27 shows what authority looks like when it is seized rather than received.**

Nothing more is required for the chapter to function.  
Nothing less explains its consequences.
